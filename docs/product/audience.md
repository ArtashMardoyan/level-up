# Audience

> **Derived from: Product Model §3 (Who is it for), §4 (What problems does it solve).**

Who Level Up is for and the problems they bring. Organized from the
[Product Model](PRODUCT_MODEL.md); no audience or problem appears here that is not in §3–§4.

## Who it is for

**Software engineers, junior through senior, preparing for technical interviews**, across
the eight tracks (§3).

**Defining segment — non-native-English-speaking developers interviewing in English.**
The trilingual product (English, Russian, Armenian) and the Dictionary's communication
coaching exist for them. Their challenge is two-fold: *know the answer*, and *say it well
under pressure* (§3).

## The problems they bring

Straight from §4:

| Problem | What the user needs |
|---|---|
| No realistic place to practice | A place that behaves like a real interview |
| Can't self-assess; no feedback | An objective rubric score + coaching + a model answer |
| Knowing ≠ articulating (esp. non-native English) | Communication coaching — vocabulary, pronunciation, phrasing |
| No structured, personalized path | A curated track now; a personalized path as the ecosystem matures |
| Motivation & consistency | Visible progress and encouragement |

How each need is met by a module is mapped in [`feature-map.md`](feature-map.md); the path
a user walks through them is in [`user-journey.md`](user-journey.md).
